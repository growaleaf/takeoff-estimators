Why a construction-estimating team built a cost game

      
The Hive builds AI tools for MEP estimating and preconstruction — and the one rule underneath all of them is the same rule underneath this game: cite the source, refuse to fabricate. A number you can't stand behind is worse than no number.

      

        

    


    
    

      
About the data

      
Every project here is real and its cost comes from a retrievable public source — GSA prospectuses, government and university capital disclosures, the owner's own figures, or trade press (ENR, Construction Dive, Architectural Record). The source is shown on every answer screen and links out so you can check it.

      
    
        
  • Total project cost is the guessed number — the all-in figure as reported, not construction hard cost alone. Implied $/SF is that total ÷ gross floor area.
    • 
        
  • "GSA-authorized" figures are the official Estimated Total Project Cost from a federal prospectus (a budget, not an as-built final) — labeled as such.
    • 
        
  • Ranges (e.g., a stadium whose cost blurs into a wider campus) are shown as a range, not a single invented number.
    • 
      

      

        What we left out — on purpose
        
Several famous projects were excluded because a clean, honestly-attributable cost-and-area pair couldn't be found (a widely-quoted "square footage" that was really a site area or an LED-screen area; a cost that was really a whole-campus figure; a total that folded in a non-construction endowment). Dropping what we can't cite is the point — it's the same discipline behind the Hive's MEP tools.
